The problem

Great proposals are how Elchai wins work. Building them doesn't scale with headcount.

Every new engagement — a DeFi platform for a bank, a computer-vision line for a manufacturer, a tokenization pilot for a real-estate group — needs its own landing page, its own deck, its own technical narrative. Right now that's a designer, a writer, and a solutions architect, working in parallel, for two to four days, per proposal.

The solution

An open-source agent swarm drafts. OpenClaw decides what leaves the building.

Kimi K2.6 — Moonshot AI's open-weight model — can split one brief across dozens of specialised sub-agents working in parallel: one drafts landing-page copy, one builds the layout, one assembles the slide deck, one writes the technical scope. Elchai already runs OpenClaw as its governance layer for exactly this kind of execution-heavy, judgment-light work. Proposal Studio is that pairing, applied to BD's biggest bottleneck.

01

Brief goes in structured input

A BD lead fills a short form: client, industry, scope, tone, budget band, must-mention proof points. No prompt engineering required.

02

Swarm drafts in parallel Kimi K2.6

Sub-agents work concurrently: one writes headline/subhead/CTA copy, one lays out a one-page landing concept, one drafts a slide outline with speaker notes, one pulls relevant case studies from Elchai's own portfolio for social proof.

03

Governance gate OpenClaw seal

A named reviewer sees a diff-style summary of what the swarm produced, checks claims against reality, fixes tone, and either approves, edits inline, or bounces it back with notes. Nothing is client-facing until this step clears.

04

Delivery & audit trail logged

Approved landing page, deck, and brief are exported and logged with who approved what and when — useful for compliance-sensitive clients like banking and government.
